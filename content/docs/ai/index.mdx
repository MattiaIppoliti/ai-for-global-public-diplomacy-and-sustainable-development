> Management Course on Adoption Strategy, Policy, and the Use of Generative AI Tools for Individual Productivity.

This course gives Luiss management and staff a shared, working understanding of artificial intelligence — what it is, what it is not, how to adopt it responsibly under EU regulation, and how to use the tools the University has approved to be more productive in day-to-day work.

<Callout type="info">
  **Course thesis** — AI can be a powerful compass, but humans are the ones who set the course.
</Callout>

## Who this course is for

Management and staff at Luiss Guido Carli who need to:

- Make informed decisions about adopting AI in their office or function.
- Use approved tools (Gemini, Microsoft 365 Copilot, NotebookLM, Lara) effectively and safely.
- Understand the obligations imposed by GDPR, the EU AI Act, and the Data Act.
- Distinguish hype from substance when evaluating new AI capabilities.

## Learning objectives

By the end of the course you should be able to:

1. Explain the difference between AI, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Generative AI to a non-technical colleague.
2. Recognize the failure modes of generative models — hallucinations, bias, the fluency effect, confirmation bias.
3. Classify an AI use case into the AI Act's risk tiers (Open / Disclosure / Compliance / Cautious / Prohibited).
4. Write a prompt that produces a usable result on the first try, using the Persona–Context–Task–Format framework.
5. Choose the right approved tool for a given task and know when *not* to upload sensitive data.

## How to use this documentation

Each section is self-contained and can be read in any order, but the sequence above is the recommended path for first-time readers. The appendix is optional — read it only if you want the mechanics behind the user-facing concepts.

<Callout type="warn">
  This course covers University-approved tools only. Do **not** use personal free-tier AI accounts (e.g. personal ChatGPT, free DeepL, free Read AI) for work involving personal, business, or otherwise confidential data. See [Ethics & Security → Key aspects](/ai/ethics-security#key-aspects-to-consider).
</Callout>

The strategy section of the course is intentionally short. Strategy is not a list of products to buy, it is a small set of principles that should let any office, faculty member or staff member make a defensible decision on their own.

## Strategic posture

Luiss's working posture on AI rests on four commitments.

### 1. Human in the loop, always

AI is for **augmentation**, not delegation. Every output that leaves the University with our name on it has been read, evaluated and signed off by a human. See the [Human-in-the-loop rule](/ai/ethics-security#5-human-in-the-loop) in Section 2.

### 2. Approved tools by default, free tools never

The University centrally procures, approves and pays for a small, well-governed set of generative AI tools. These are the only tools to be used with information that is personal, confidential, proprietary or otherwise sensitive.

The approved set covered in this course is:

- **Lara by Translated**, translations.
- **Google Gemini**, individual productivity, drafting, learning, deep research.
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat**, included with Microsoft 365; covers light productivity tasks.
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot** (paid license, €137/year), full integration with Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, OneNote; agents, data analysis, report building.
- **Google NotebookLM**, turning your own documents into podcasts, video summaries, mind maps, reports, flashcards, quizzes, infographics, and slides.
- **Copilot Studio / Google Gems**, building lightweight agents on top of the above.

See [Section 4 → Tools](/ai/applications#tools-for-using-ai-effectively) for the catalogue and when to use each.

<Callout type="warn">
  Avoid free or personal AI solutions for work activities, including personal ChatGPT, free DeepL or Read AI. Data you enter into a free product may be used to train the vendor's model.
</Callout>

### 3. Adoption follows a risk-based approach

Every proposed AI use case at the University is sorted into the AI Act's four bands ([Open / Disclosure / Compliance / Cautious / Prohibited](/ai/ethics-security#a-risk-based-approach-for-the-university)) **before** it is approved.

The University may go *stricter* than the regulatory floor. A use that the AI Act would classify as *Cautious-but-allowed* can still be banned internally if the institutional risk is judged too high (e.g. student-selection algorithms, misconduct prediction).

### 4. Literacy first

Tools without literacy create more risk than they remove. Luiss invests in **AI literacy**, this course, the AI Handbook, follow-up workshops, so that every member of staff carries the framework with them, not just access credentials.

The target proficiency profile for management is laid out in [Section 2 → AI Literacy Framework](/ai/ethics-security#the-ai-literacy-framework). The two non-negotiable dimensions are **Critical Thinking** and **Ethical & Responsible Use**.

## Where the strategy lives

This is a living strategy. The authoritative reference for current policies, approved tools, and updates is the Luiss AI Handbook.

<Callout type="info">
  **Luiss AI Handbook**, [aihandbook.luiss.it](https://aihandbook.luiss.it/it/)
</Callout>

The handbook is the place to check before:

- adopting a new AI vendor;
- enabling an AI feature on existing software;
- processing personal or sensitive data through an AI tool;
- publishing any AI-generated content under the University's name.

If a situation is not covered by the handbook, it should be escalated, not improvised.

## A practical decision flow

Before using AI for a task at work, walk through this short check.

1. **Is the task in scope?** If it involves prohibited use cases ([AI Act tier 4](/ai/ethics-security#4--prohibited-use-unacceptable-risk)), stop.
2. **Is the data sensitive?** If yes, only use **University-approved tools**. Never paste sensitive data into a free or personal account.
3. **What is the risk tier?** Use the [risk-based approach](/ai/ethics-security#a-risk-based-approach-for-the-university) table to classify the use case.
4. **Do I need disclosure?** If the AI output will be read by another human, indicate clearly that AI was involved.
5. **Have I verified the output?** Cross-check facts and figures against grounded sources. Do not ship anything you have not read.
6. **Have I kept the prompt?** Keep a copy of the prompt used to generate the content, for transparency and IP traceability.

If any step is unclear, refer to the AI Handbook or escalate before publishing.

## Key takeaways

1. Strategy = **Human-in-the-loop · Approved tools · Risk-based adoption · Literacy first**.
2. The approved-tool list is small on purpose, central procurement enables central governance.
3. The University's policy can be stricter than EU law, never looser.
4. When in doubt: AI Handbook → escalate → only then act.
